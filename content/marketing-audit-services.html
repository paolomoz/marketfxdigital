Common problems we find

The patterns that quietly cap growth

Almost every audit surfaces a version of these. The cost is rarely one big leak — it is a dozen small ones compounding across the funnel.

Want to go deeper on a specific layer? See our SEO and AI visibility consulting, paid media services, and marketing analytics and reporting.

Wasted media spend

Budget concentrated in campaigns, audiences, or placements that look healthy in-platform but never convert into pipeline or revenue.

Weak tracking

Missing server-side events, blocked pixels, duplicated conversions, and dashboards that quietly disagree with the CRM.

Duplicate conversion goals

The same action counted across multiple platforms, inflating reported performance and corrupting bidding signals.

Poor landing page alignment

Ads promise one thing, landing pages deliver another — and the bounce rate proves it.

Fragmented vendors

Three to six agencies optimizing their own slice while no one owns the full-funnel outcome.

Low organic visibility

Thin service pages, weak schema, and no defensible topical authority — invisible in both Google and AI engines.

No clear reporting framework

Different numbers in every meeting, no single source of truth, and no leading indicators tied to revenue.

Missing CRM follow up

Leads captured and then dropped — no scoring, no nurture, no handoff SLA, and no closed-loop reporting.

Thin content

Pages written for keywords instead of buyers — and neither Google nor AI engines find anything worth citing.

Weak location-level visibility

Inconsistent NAP data, neglected Google Business Profiles, and location pages that all look the same to search engines.

FAQ

Marketing audits, answered

What is a full-funnel marketing audit?

A full-funnel marketing audit is a structured review of every channel and system that influences revenue — paid media, SEO, AI visibility, website conversion, CRM, analytics, creative, and reporting. The goal is to identify what is helping growth, what is holding it back, and the prioritized actions that will move the number.

How is this different from a paid media audit or an SEO audit alone?

A paid media audit or SEO audit looks at one channel in isolation. A full-funnel marketing audit looks at how every channel, tracking system, and revenue page works together — because most growth problems live in the seams between channels, not inside any single one.

How long does a marketing audit take?

Most engagements run two to four weeks from kickoff to final deliverable, depending on scope, account complexity, and access. Quick-win recommendations are shared as soon as they are found so execution can start before the full report lands.

What access do you need from our team?

Read-only access to your ad accounts, GA4, server-side tracking platform, CRM, CMS, search console, and any business intelligence dashboards. We provide a clear access checklist at kickoff and never request credentials outside of the platforms' standard sharing flows.

Will the audit disrupt our current campaigns?

No. The audit is read-only by default. We do not pause campaigns, change bids, or alter tracking during the review. Any changes are proposed in the recommendations and only executed after you approve scope and sequencing.

Do you audit work done by our existing agencies?

Yes — and we do it neutrally. The audit assesses outcomes, not relationships. The deliverable tells you which partners are producing measurable value, where the work is duplicative, and where a different operating model would unlock growth.

What does an enterprise marketing audit cost?

Audit fees are scoped to the size and complexity of the marketing system — number of channels, locations, ad accounts, and CRM depth. We share a fixed fee in writing after a brief scoping call so there are no surprises mid-engagement.

What happens after the audit?

You own the deliverable and can execute it however you choose. Most clients ask marketFX to lead execution on at least the analytics and SEO and AI visibility workstreams, with the rest distributed across in-house teams or existing partners against the 90-day roadmap.
